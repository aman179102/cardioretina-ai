# Problem Statement: Cardiovascular Risk Screening

## The Challenge

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) remains the **leading cause of mortality worldwide**, accounting for approximately 17.9 million deaths annually according to the World Health Organization.

### Current Screening Limitations

#### 1. Invasive Methods
- **Cardiac catheterization**: Requires inserting a catheter into blood vessels
- **Stress tests**: Physically demanding and not suitable for all patients
- **Blood draws**: Require trained personnel and laboratory facilities

#### 2. Expensive Technology
- **CT angiography**: High cost limits accessibility
- **MRI**: Expensive equipment and maintenance
- **Echocardiography**: Requires specialized technicians

#### 3. Accessibility Barriers
- Rural and underserved areas lack cardiac imaging facilities
- Long wait times for specialist appointments
- Requires transportation to medical centers

### The Retinal Connection

Research has established that the **retinal microvasculature** shares:
- **Embryological origins** with coronary vessels
- **Physiological properties** with heart vasculature
- **Biomarker signals** for systemic cardiovascular health

#### Key Research Findings

1. **Poplin et al. (2018)**: Demonstrated that deep learning can predict cardiovascular risk factors from retinal images with accuracy comparable to traditional risk calculators

2. **Wong & Mitchell (2004)**: Established the connection between hypertensive retinopathy and cardiovascular disease

3. **Recent Studies**: Show that retinal vessel caliber, tortuosity, and branching patterns correlate with:
   - Coronary artery disease
   - Stroke risk
   - Hypertension severity
   - Diabetes complications

### The Opportunity

Retinal fundus imaging offers:
- **Non-invasive**: Simple photography, no discomfort
- **Low-cost**: Fundus cameras are far cheaper than cardiac imaging
- **Widely available**: Many clinics and even some pharmacies have cameras
- **Quick**: Takes only seconds to capture
- **Safe**: No radiation or invasive procedures

### Project Goal

CardioRetina-AI aims to demonstrate how deep learning can:
1. Extract cardiovascular risk signals from retinal images
2. Combine image features with clinical data for improved accuracy
3. Provide explainable predictions for research transparency
4. Serve as an educational tool for medical AI development

### Target Use Cases

- **Research**: Validating retinal-cardiovascular associations
- **Education**: Teaching AI in healthcare contexts
- **Screening Research**: Investigating non-invasive alternatives
- **Technology Demonstration**: Showcasing multimodal AI capabilities
